Parkinson’s Disease:
Why Movement Becomes Difficult

  • Parkinson’s disease (PD) = neurodegenerative disorder
  • Loss of dopamine-producing neurons in the substantia nigra
  • Disrupts the basal ganglia, which help coordinate movement
  • Patients often lose 60–80% of cells before diagnosis
  • Core symptom: Freezing of Gait (FOG)
The Nigrostriatal Pathway: Dopamine depletion leads to motor dysfunction.

What Does Freezing
of Gait Look Like?

The "Feet Glued to the Floor" phenomenon is often triggered by doorways or narrow spaces.

Typical Triggers

  • Starting to walk (Hesitation)
  • Turning in tight spaces
  • Navigating doorways
  • Dual-tasking (Doing two things at once)
  • Stress or anxiety

Clinical Case Study

68-year-old patient, 9 years post-diagnosis. Experiences frequent "glued" episodes at doorways, leading to recent near-falls and loss of confidence.
